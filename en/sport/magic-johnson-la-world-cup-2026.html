NBA legend urges fans to make Los Angeles their World Cup destination

Magic Johnson urged fans around the world to make Los Angeles their World Cup destination, framing the city as a global sports capital.

May 13, 20264 minFootball

NBA legend Magic Johnson urged fans to make Los Angeles their destination for the 2026 FIFA World Cup.

He framed the city as a global sports capital and argued that the tournament should become more than a series of football matches: a citywide festival for international fans.

What Los Angeles is preparing

  • 8 tournament matches in the region
  • large weekend fan festivals
  • transport plans for visitors
  • promotion led by athletes and local celebrities

SoFi Stadium is set to be one of the tournament’s key venues, while Los Angeles is already trying to turn the World Cup into a major tourism moment.

Why it matters

The 2026 World Cup will be the largest in history, hosted by the United States, Mexico and Canada with 48 teams. For Los Angeles, it is a chance to make football part of the city’s mainstream sports culture.

Pereuloq view

World Cup 2026 will not only be a sports tournament. It will also be a competition between cities for global attention, and Los Angeles is already moving early.
