What is happening in the world now — key events for May 14, 2026

Key events for May 14, 2026: U.S.-China talks, Elon Musk in Beijing, Iran tensions, Ukraine and Russia, and the global race for the future of AI.

By Almir KhialovMay 14, 20267 min readWorld politics

On May 14, the global agenda has folded into one large picture: the United States and China began the biggest talks of the year, Elon Musk appeared close to diplomacy and business, tension around Iran is pushing oil higher again, Ukraine and Russia remain central to the news cycle, and the race for the future of AI has become the main technological front.

The United States and China begin the biggest talks of the year

Donald Trump arrived in Beijing for a two-day summit with Xi Jinping. The agenda includes Taiwan, Iran, global trade, artificial intelligence, technology, sanctions and investment.

  • Xi Jinping said the United States and China should be partners, not rivals.
  • Trump described relations between the two countries as fantastic.
  • The larger backdrop is the race for chips, AI infrastructure and new supply chains.

Elon Musk returns to the center of global politics

Elon Musk arrived in China with the American delegation and is taking part in meetings between the United States and China. Tim Cook, Larry Fink and executives from Boeing, Qualcomm, Goldman Sachs and BlackRock are also in China.

The signal is clear: despite political conflict, the largest corporations are trying to preserve ties between the United States and China. For business, China remains too important to be removed from strategy.

Tension around Iran rises again

The ceasefire between the United States and Iran remains unstable. The Pentagon says it is ready to escalate if necessary, Iran is threatening to increase uranium enrichment, and oil prices continue to rise.

Brent has moved above $107 per barrel, increasing pressure on inflation, logistics, air travel and European energy markets.

Ukraine and Russia

Kyiv faced a major overnight attack involving missiles and drones. Residential buildings were damaged, with deaths and dozens of injuries reported.

At the same time, the Kremlin says the conflict is moving toward completion and that a meeting between Vladimir Putin and Volodymyr Zelenskyy would only make sense for finalizing a peace process.

The main global theme is the fight for the future of AI

The world is effectively divided between two technological superpowers: the United States, with OpenAI, Nvidia, Tesla and Microsoft, and China, with DeepSeek, Tencent, Huawei and BYD.

  • artificial intelligence
  • robots
  • chip manufacturing
  • autonomous systems
  • military technology

Pereuloq view

That is why the Trump-Xi meeting matters more than most other world events right now. This is no longer just diplomacy. It is a contest over the rules of the future economy, the internet, AI and technological security.
