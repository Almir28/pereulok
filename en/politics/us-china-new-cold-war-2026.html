The world enters a new cold war as the US and China prepare for major 2026 talks

Talks between Donald Trump and Xi Jinping unfold amid trade tensions, the AI race, Iran, Taiwan and a restructuring global economy.

By Almir KhialovMay 13, 20266 minUS and China

The world is watching talks between Donald Trump and Xi Jinping in Beijing. It is one of the biggest US-China meetings in years.

The meeting comes amid trade tensions, the AI race, the Iran crisis, pressure around Taiwan and a global economic reset.

Why the meeting matters

The United States and China remain the two central forces of the global economy. In recent years, relations have worsened sharply: tariffs rose, companies faced sanctions, chip exports were restricted and AI became part of geopolitics.

What leaders are discussing

  • trade tariffs
  • rare earth exports
  • artificial intelligence
  • Taiwan
  • the Iran crisis
  • energy security

Why it affects ordinary people

These talks can affect technology prices, car costs, electronics supply, AI services, oil and jobs. In practice, the next decade of the internet, technology and the economy is being shaped now.

Pereuloq view

Earlier cold wars were built around weapons. The new cold war is being built around AI, chips, data, technology and control over the global economy.
