Europe braces for a second energy shock as the 2026 crisis intensifies

Europe faces rising oil and gas prices, pressure on industry and emergency measures as the Hormuz crisis shakes energy markets.

By Almir KhialovMay 13, 20266 minEconomy

Europe is feeling the consequences of the Iran crisis more sharply. After attacks on energy infrastructure and disruption around the Strait of Hormuz, oil and gas prices have jumped.

Analysts are already describing the situation as a second energy shock after the 2022 crisis.

What is happening

The Strait of Hormuz is one of the world’s most important energy routes. Large oil shipments, LNG flows and critical logistics chains for Europe and Asia pass through it.

Germany and Britain are already under pressure

Germany is seeing pressure on wholesale prices, especially fuel, transport, industrial logistics and raw materials. In the United Kingdom, investors fear another wave of inflation and slower growth.

The EU is preparing emergency measures

The European Commission is discussing lower energy taxes, coordination of gas reserves, faster alternative energy deployment and protection for industry against another price shock.

Pereuloq view

2026 is showing that energy is no longer only economics. It is becoming one of the world’s central political weapons. The next few months may define how deep the new global crisis becomes.
