Apple is preparing its biggest iPhone AI update — what to expect at WWDC 2026

Apple is preparing a major Apple Intelligence update for iOS with a new Siri, offline AI, smarter summaries and a system-level personal assistant.

By Almir KhialovMay 13, 20265 minTechnology

While the market is focused on OpenAI and new AI video tools, Apple is preparing an update that could reshape the iPhone experience this year.

According to industry insiders and analysts, WWDC 2026 may bring a major Apple Intelligence update that becomes a central part of the next iOS release. Apple’s goal is to make AI fast, quiet and deeply integrated into everyday actions.

What could appear

  • A next-generation Siri with more natural conversation
  • Automatic photo and video editing
  • AI summaries for notifications and messages
  • Smarter battery management powered by machine learning
  • Text generation inside Notes, Mail and Safari
  • A system-level personal AI assistant

Apple is also reportedly working on offline AI, so some features can run without an internet connection. That could become one of the company’s clearest differences from competitors.

Why it matters

AI is now the fastest-growing category on the internet, but most tools still live in separate apps, websites or subscriptions. Apple wants to build intelligence directly into the iPhone so users barely notice the system working, but feel the result every day.

What it means for users

For everyday users, this means fewer manual actions, faster workflows, a smarter iPhone and new features without installing dozens of extra apps. For the industry, it signals a new stage of the AI race between Apple, OpenAI, Google and Microsoft.

Pereuloq view

Apple rarely arrives first. But when it arrives later, it often changes how people actually use a technology. That is why WWDC 2026 could become one of the year’s most important tech events.
